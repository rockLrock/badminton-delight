Badminton History

Modern badminton grew out of older shuttlecock games played across Asia and Europe. In the mid-19th century British officers in India popularised a racket-and-shuttlecock game called "Poona"; returning players brought the game to England, where the Bath Badminton Club and others helped standardise the rules in the 1870s. The sport's name likely comes from Badminton House in Gloucestershire, where the game was played socially.

Throughout the 20th century badminton expanded globally, with particularly strong traditions developing in many Asian and European countries. The International Badminton Federation (now Badminton World Federation) formed in 1934, and badminton became an Olympic sport in 1992. Today it is played both recreationally and professionally, prized for its fast pace, strategy, and accessibility.

About Us

Badminton Delight is a community of badminton enthusiasts based in London Regent's Park. We are dedicated to promoting the sport of badminton, fostering a friendly and inclusive environment for players of all skill levels. Whether you're a beginner looking to learn the basics or an experienced player seeking competitive matches, Badminton Delight has something for everyone.
